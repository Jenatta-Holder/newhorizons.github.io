Salary and Working Hours

A Petroleum Engineer working in Guyana will typically earn around 2,086,500 GYD per year, and this can range from the lowest average salary of about 1,023,000 GYD to the highest average salary of 3,263,500 GYD.

Petroleum engineers are expected to work full time up to as much as 50 to 60 hours every week. This is especially true when they are onsite. They typically work in shifts or rotation such as 84 hours on duty and 84 hours off.

Salary for Experience

  • 0 - 2 Years Experience - A Petroleum Engineer in Guyana that has less than two years of experience can expect to earn somewhere in the region of 1,212,800 GYD.
  • 2 - 5 Years Experience - With two to five years of experience the average Petroleum Engineer salary would increase to 1,560,800 GYD.
  • 5 - 10 Years Experience - From five to ten years of experience as a Petroleum Engineer , the average salary would be 2,146,100 GYD.
  • 10 - 15 Years Experience - Once you have more than ten years of experience the average salary reaches around 2,662,900 GYD.
  • 15 - 20 Years Experience - A Petroleum Engineer with 15 to 20 years of experience can earn an average of 2,854,700 GYD.
  • 20+ Years Experience - For a Petroleum Engineer with more than 20 years, the expected average salary increases to 3,047,800 GYD.

Salary for Education Levels

  • Bachelor's Degree. A Petroleum Engineer in Guyana with a bachelor's degree education can earn an average salary of 1,510,400 GYD.
  • Master's Degree. A Petroleum Engineer in Guyana with a master's degree education can earn an average salary of 2,435,600 GYD.

Salary for Gender

In Guyana, a male petroleum engineer will earn an average of 2,161,200 GYD, while a female petroleum engineer will earn around 1,990,300 GYD.
This means that a male petroleum engineer earns approximately 9% more than a female petroleum engineer for performing the same job.
